JELLYFISH 
The jellyfish embodies a plurality of meanings: They embody the 'more-than-human ocean' as they thrive in seas where anthropogenic action is lethal to most other species.
They are master adapters in terms of habitats and diet. Their tentacles are also a metaphor: they represent the complex interactions involved in marine ecosystems and eco-social crises. This character focuses on contamination of the Hydrosphere as it direct links between local water struggles within the broader context of marine ecosystems, illustrating the interconnectedness of hydrospheric health and socio-political activism.

In response to the constraints of extractive digital economies and dominant archival regimes, the Non-Archives case study traces the possibilities of experimental publishing to disrupt and reframe the politics of documentation. The project opens provocations on how to correspond lived and digital experiences.

Through the building of a navigable 3D space using the Godot game engine and the Ocean Archive API, the proposal of the case study questions what an archive becomes when it is spatialized and relational to the other contents. Developed as an immersive experiment, its content is experienced as unfolding, entangled, and situated. As players move through the digitial terrain of the archive where histories are shaped, silences are made, and resistance can be mobilized. It proposes the archive not as an enclosed space but as a terrain grounded in the lived experiences and political urgencies.
